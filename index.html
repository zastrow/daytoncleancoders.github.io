Dayton Clean Coders

Exploring patterns, practices, and tools for sustainable code

Dayton Clean Coders is was a monthly meetup of developers who have a desire to share, practice and contribute to clean code.

The times, they are a changin'

Four years ago at this time I was in the giddy stage of organizing a new user group called Cincy Clean Coders. I was looking forward to a different kind of group where devs could explore patterns, practices, and tools for sustainable code. Code agnostic, I was excited for less talking heads and more experimentation, exploration, and banter.

Not long after that, the Sparkbox team and I started Dayton Clean Coders, which eventually led to my joining their team full time (yeah!). I've learned an immense amount from the participants of both CCC and DCC, and I hope the same goes for those who've joined us.

Starting in 2015, Dayton Clean Coders will be going on hiatus with no immediate plans to reorganize. At Sparkbox we're continuing our efforts on the Build Right series of workshops, adding Build Right: Collaboration and Build Right: Frontend Testing to the immensely popular Build Right: Build Responsively, Build Right: Frontend Tooling, and Build Right: Maker Series. Gem City JS will continue to meet the 3rd Thursday of each month with an awesome lineup already scheduled for much of 2015. We're also looking into a few other ideas that have come up over the last few months.

I'm amazingly excited to see another group of passionate geeks looking to share their thoughts and ideas with the greater Dayton area. I'll be sitting down with their team to talk about what we learned over the years, and our team would be happy to do so with others who are interested. If you have thoughts let me or the Pomiet team know. Or... kick off your own event! Sparkbox has your back.
